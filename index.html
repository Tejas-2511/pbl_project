PortionVision

"An intelligent nutrition system for hostel mess environments that combines personalized portion recommendations with image-based portion estimation for accurate comparison."

Problem Statement

In hostel mess environments, food is self-served by students without structured guidance on portion sizes. Since students differ in age, height, weight, metabolism, activity level, and health goals, they often rely on guesswork, leading to over-consumption or under-consumption of nutrients. Manual calorie tracking is also inaccurate, and estimating food portions from a single RGB image is challenging due to the lack of depth information, especially for Indian dishes like rice, dal, sabzi, and curries.

Literature Survey Highlights

Depth Anything V2, Marigold, and MiDaS for monocular depth estimation, SAM/SAM2 for segmentation, and IFCT as the primary Indian nutrition database reference.

Research Gap

Most nutrition apps depend on manual food logging and do not support automated portion estimation, making them unsuitable for Indian hostel mess meals where dishes are mixed and portions are not standardized.

Novelty / Key Contribution

PortionVision integrates menu digitization, personalized portion recommendations, and monocular depth-based estimation to compare recommended intake with actual consumption.

Proposed Solution Workflow

Menu Digitization

Image Upload -> Sharp Preprocessing -> Tesseract.js OCR -> Cleaning & Deduplication -> JSON Database .

Personalized Recommendation

User Profile Input -> BMI & BMR Calculation -> Daily Calorie & Protein Requirement -> Portion Recommendation Logic.

Image-Based Estimation

Depth estimation -> segmentation -> plate calibration -> depth-to-volume conversion -> density-based mass estimation -> nutrition mapping.
