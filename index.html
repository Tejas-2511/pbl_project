PortionVision

"An intelligent nutrition system for hostel mess environments that combines personalized portion recommendations with image-based portion estimation for accurate comparison."

Problem Statement

In hostel mess environments, food is self-served by students, but there is no structured guidance on appropriate portion sizes based on individual nutritional requirements. Students differ in age, height, weight, metabolic rate, activity level, dietary preference, and health goals, yet they rely on personal judgment when selecting quantities. This often leads to under-consumption or over-consumption of essential nutrients. Additionally, traditional calorie-tracking methods depend on manual estimation, which is subjective and inaccurate. Estimating precise portion size from a single RGB image is also challenging due to the absence of explicit depth and volume information, particularly for irregular Indian dishes such as rice, dal, sabzi, and curries.

Literature Survey Highlights

Depth Anything V2, Marigold, and MiDaS for monocular depth estimation. SAM and SAM 2.0 for segmentation. IFCT 2017 as the primary Indian nutrition database.

Research Gap

Existing calorie apps rely on manual input and lack 3D portion estimation, making them unsuitable for Indian mess-based nutrition monitoring.

Proposed Solution Workflow

Menu Digitization

Image Upload -> Sharp Preprocessing -> Tesseract.js OCR -> Cleaning & Deduplication -> JSON Database Update.

Personalized Recommendation

User Profile Input -> BMI & BMR Calculation -> Daily Calorie & Protein Requirement -> Planned Portion Recommendation Logic.

Image-Based Estimation

Depth estimation -> segmentation -> plate calibration -> volume -> mass -> nutrition mapping.
